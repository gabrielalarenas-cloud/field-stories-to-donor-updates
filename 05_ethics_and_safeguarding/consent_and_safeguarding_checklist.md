# Consent and Safeguarding Checklist

## Purpose

This checklist is designed to prevent harm when using AI voice and dubbing tools for NGO storytelling.

It should be completed before any field story, image, audio, video, or donor-facing narrative is published.

## Core principle

AI should help reduce operational burden and improve accessibility. It should not weaken consent, exaggerate impact, or make vulnerable people easier to exploit through more polished storytelling.

## Section 1: Content source

Before using any material, confirm:

- [ ] The content is fictional, public, or approved for use
- [ ] The source of the content is documented
- [ ] No personal data is included without permission
- [ ] No child or vulnerable person is identifiable without appropriate consent
- [ ] The field team understands how the material may be used
- [ ] The content is appropriate for donor-facing communication

## Section 2: Consent

Confirm:

- [ ] Consent was collected before recording or sharing the story
- [ ] The person giving consent understood the purpose of the content
- [ ] The person understood where the content may appear
- [ ] Consent includes translation, narration, or dubbing if applicable
- [ ] Consent includes use in donor communications if applicable
- [ ] Consent can be withdrawn where required
- [ ] Consent records are stored securely

## Section 3: Voice and likeness

Confirm:

- [ ] No person's voice is cloned without explicit written consent
- [ ] No beneficiary voice is recreated synthetically without approval
- [ ] No child voice is cloned or simulated
- [ ] No staff voice is cloned for convenience without consent
- [ ] Any AI-generated voice is clearly disclosed when appropriate
- [ ] Synthetic narration does not imply that a beneficiary personally said words they did not say

## Section 4: Story accuracy

Confirm:

- [ ] Program facts are accurate
- [ ] Numbers and outcomes are verified
- [ ] Translations preserve meaning
- [ ] The story does not exaggerate impact
- [ ] The donor-facing version does not remove important context
- [ ] A program owner has reviewed the final version
- [ ] A communications owner has reviewed the final version

## Section 5: Dignity-first storytelling

Confirm:

- [ ] The story avoids pity-based framing
- [ ] The person or community is represented with dignity
- [ ] The story does not reduce people to hardship
- [ ] The story includes agency, context, or progress where possible
- [ ] The image or voice use would not embarrass or endanger the person represented
- [ ] The story would still feel respectful if the person featured saw it

## Section 6: Safeguarding risk

Flag the story for additional review if it includes:

- Children
- Medical information
- Displacement, migration, or legal status
- Violence or abuse
- Conflict zones
- Sensitive locations
- Stigmatized health conditions
- Financial hardship
- Identifiable family details
- Any situation where public exposure could create risk

## Section 7: Publication approval

Before publication:

- [ ] Field team has reviewed the meaning
- [ ] Program lead has reviewed the accuracy
- [ ] Safeguarding lead has reviewed the risk
- [ ] Communications lead has reviewed the final asset
- [ ] AI-generated elements are documented
- [ ] The asset is stored in the approved location
- [ ] Publication channel is approved
- [ ] The team has a takedown process if concerns arise

## Final decision

Select one:

- [ ] Approved for publication
- [ ] Approved with edits
- [ ] Needs additional consent
- [ ] Needs safeguarding review
- [ ] Not approved for donor use
