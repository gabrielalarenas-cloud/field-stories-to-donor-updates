# Sample NGO Scenario
## Fictional pilot partner: LearningStars Education Initiative

## Disclaimer

This scenario is fictional and created only for demonstration purposes. It does not represent a real NGO, real community, real beneficiary, or real field report. It is compilations of based on my personal experience working with multiple similar initiatives. 

No real personal data, beneficiary images, or field staff recordings are used.

## Organization profile

**Organization name:** LearningStars Education Initiative
**Headquarters:** Toronto, Canada  
**Program regions:** Ecuador, Kenya, and Nepal  
**Program focus:** Girls' education and after-school literacy support  
**Primary donor base:** North America  
**Languages used in field updates:** Spanish, English, French, Swahili, Nepali  
**Primary donor communication language:** English, with selected French and Spanish donor communications  

## The operational context

LearningStars Education Initiative runs after-school literacy programs through local partner organizations.

Each month, field coordinators send program updates to the HQ fundraising and communications team.

These updates are used for:

- Donor newsletters
- Impact reports
- Fundraising campaigns
- Social media posts
- Grant updates
- Major donor stewardship

## Current workflow

```text
Field coordinator captures photos, videos, and written notes
↓
Content is sent through WhatsApp, email, or shared folders
↓
HQ team requests clarification or translation
↓
Field team responds when available
↓
Communications team drafts donor narrative
↓
Program team reviews for accuracy
↓
Leadership reviews for tone and fundraising relevance
↓
Final donor update is published
```

## Current pain points

| Pain point | Operational effect |
|---|---|
| Field staff are busy with program delivery | Updates are delayed or incomplete |
| Raw updates arrive in different formats | HQ spends time organizing material |
| Updates arrive in multiple languages | Translation slows publication |
| Beneficiary quotes may lack consent records | Publication risk increases |
| Storytelling depends on individual staff skill | Donor communications vary in quality |
| HQ requests too much extra context | Field teams feel frustrated |
| Final content arrives late | Donor updates feel less immediate |

## Fictional raw field update

A field coordinator in Ecuador records the following voice note in Spanish:

> Esta semana tuvimos 42 estudiantes en el programa de lectura después de clases. Las profesoras nos dijeron que varias niñas están leyendo en voz alta con más confianza. También empezamos los círculos de lectura con las familias los sábados. Una madre nos dijo que ahora lee con su hija en casa tres noches por semana.

## Donor-ready version

After human review, consent check, and translation, the update could become:

> This week, 42 students joined LearningStars' after-school reading sessions in rural Ecuador. Teachers reported that several girls are now reading aloud with greater confidence. The team also launched Saturday family reading circles, helping parents continue literacy support at home. One mother shared that she now reads with her daughter three nights a week.

## Use of ElevenLabs in this scenario

ElevenLabs could support the workflow by helping HQ teams:

- Convert the reviewed donor script into natural narration
- Produce English, Spanish, and French versions of the same update
- Dub short field videos for multilingual donor audiences
- Create accessible audio versions of written donor reports
- Maintain consistency across recurring updates

## Human review requirements

Before publication, the NGO must confirm:

- The data is accurate
- The story has consent
- The beneficiary is not identifiable without permission
- The narrative preserves dignity
- The field team approves the final meaning
- The AI-generated output is reviewed by a human
- The final material is appropriate for donor use

## Pilot goal

The pilot would test whether the workflow can reduce time from field update to donor-ready communication while maintaining consent, accuracy, and dignity.

## Pilot hypothesis

If field teams can submit short updates in their own language and HQ can use ElevenLabs to create reviewed multilingual donor communications, then the NGO can publish updates faster, reduce field staff burden, and improve donor understanding of program impact.
