# ElevenLabs Presentation Talk Track
## How to explain the project in 2 minutes

## Opening

I built this as a proof of concept for how ElevenLabs could help NGOs solve a real operational bottleneck: the gap between field teams who are closest to the work and HQ teams responsible for donor communication.

The project is not about replacing storytellers. It is about amplifying field knowledge through a workflow that is faster, multilingual, and still human-reviewed.

## Point 1: Human-in-the-loop

The most important design choice is that AI does not publish anything on its own.

Every update goes through consent, safeguarding, accuracy, translation, and communications review before it becomes donor-facing.

For NGOs, trust is more important than speed.

## Point 2: Storyboard as blueprint

The storyboard is not just a creative artifact.

It acts as a blueprint or creative contract.

It defines the structure, tone, review gates, voice direction, and success metrics. In a real deployment, that would help an NGO produce recurring multilingual updates consistently instead of reinventing the process every time.

## Point 3: Partnership scaling potential

The bigger opportunity is not one video.

It is a repeatable deployment model.

If this workflow worked for one NGO country office, it could be adapted across regions, languages, program types, and donor segments.

For a large global NGO, that could mean faster local-to-global communication with stronger consent controls and less burden on field staff.

## Point 4: Emotional consistency

The narrator voice is designed to sound warm, credible, and calm.

The point is not to make the story more dramatic. The point is to preserve the human context of the message across languages without exaggerating the work or flattening the local meaning.

## Point 5: Multilingual moment

In the multilingual section, I would call attention to the fact that the value is not only translation.

The value is preserving the meaning, tone, and trust of the message across languages.

That is what makes this relevant for global nonprofit partnerships.

## Closing

This is a small demo, but the operating question behind it is the real point: how do you turn an AI capability into a responsible, repeatable workflow that partners can actually adopt?

That is the kind of work I am excited to do at ElevenLabs.
