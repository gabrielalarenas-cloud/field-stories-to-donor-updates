# Narrator Voice Persona
## Emotional consistency and brand trust

## Purpose

Voice choice matters in NGO storytelling.

The narrator should not sound overly dramatic, sales-driven, or emotionally manipulative. The voice should convey empathy, authority, clarity, and trust.

This file defines the voice persona for the demo and provides guidance for selecting or directing an ElevenLabs voice.

## Voice persona

**Working name:** Janet  
**Role:** Donor update narrator  
**Tone:** Warm, credible, calm, emotionally grounded  
**Audience:** Donors, NGO staff, program partners, and impact stakeholders  
**Emotional goal:** Help the listener understand the human meaning of the update without exaggerating or exploiting the story  

## Voice characteristics

| Characteristic | Direction |
|---|---|
| Pace | Moderate, clear, not rushed |
| Emotion | Warm but restrained |
| Authority | Confident and trustworthy |
| Energy | Hopeful, not promotional |
| Clarity | High pronunciation accuracy across languages |
| Style | Human, documentary-like, respectful |

## What the voice should avoid

- Overly dramatic delivery
- Fundraising urgency that feels manipulative
- Pity-based tone
- Corporate monotone
- Synthetic enthusiasm
- Any suggestion that the narrator is the field worker or beneficiary

## Brand logic

For NGOs, voice is part of trust.

A good voice should make the donor feel closer to the work while still respecting the people represented in the story.

In this demo, voice and music should work together to create emotional consistency:

- The voice carries credibility and care
- The music supports focus and warmth
- The script avoids exaggeration
- The visuals remain respectful
- The workflow keeps consent and review visible

## Music direction

Use light, minimal background music only if it improves clarity.

Recommended: soft piano or ambient instrumental, low volume, no dramatic crescendos, no overly sentimental soundtrack, and no music that makes hardship feel cinematic.

## Multilingual direction

For the multilingual section, the listener should feel that the same message carries across languages with consistent tone.

Recommended approach:

1. Generate the same short message in English
2. Generate the same message in Spanish
3. Test one additional global language segment, such as Swahili, with native-speaker review
4. Compare tone, pacing, and emotional consistency
5. Do not publish any language version for real use without human review

## Disclosure

If the voice is AI-generated, disclose it where appropriate.

Example:

> This demo uses fictional NGO content and AI-generated narration for proof-of-concept purposes.
